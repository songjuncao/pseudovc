PseudoVC: Improving One-shot Voice Conversion with Pseudo Paired Data

Paper Project

Abstract

The voice conversion (VC) task usually contains two key parts, which are the content encoder and the speaker encoder. Waveform reconstruction is usually performed during training phase. However, the mismatch between training and inference hinders the performance of those VC systems. To alleviate the mismatch problem, we propose a new VC training method named \textit{PseudoVC} in this paper. Firstly, we utilizes VC models to generate pseudo examples, which will be fed into the content encoder during training. This new information perturbation approach is named as \textit{pseudo examples}. Secondly, we will substitute the input to speaker model by another utterance which shares the same speaker during training, which is denoted as \textit{speaker sample}. Experimental results show that our \textit{pseudo examples} is superior to previous information perturbation approaches and the overall \textit{PseudoVC} method outperforms publicly available VC models. Audio examples are available \footnote{https://songjuncao.github.io/} and our model will release.
